# Telecom customer churn analysis

## 📋 Project Overview
This project analyzes the **Telco Customer Churn Dataset** (7,043 customers) to identify key factors driving customer churn. The analysis was performed primarily in **Microsoft Excel**, with an interactive **Power BI dashboard** for business-friendly visualizations and storytelling.
The main goal was to uncover patterns in customer behavior and provide actionable recommendations to improve customer retention.

## 🎯 Project Objectives
- Explore and clean the dataset using Microsoft Excel.
- Identify the major drivers of customer churn.
- Analyze the impact of contract type, tenure, internet service, payment method, and other variables on churn.
- Build interactive PivotTables and visualizations in Excel.
- Create a professional interactive dashboard in Power BI.
- Deliver key insights.
- Deliver data-driven business recommendations.

  ## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies Used
- **Microsoft Excel** – Data cleaning, PivotTables, Charts.
- **Power BI** – Interactive Dashboard Development.

## 📊 Dataset Information
- **Total Records**: 7,043 customers
- **Time Period**: Not specified (cross-sectional data)
- **Target Variable**: `Churn` (Yes/No)
- One additional calculated column was added for analysis: **tenure group**
  
The dataset was already clean with no duplicates.

## 🧹 Data Preparation
- Verified data quality (no duplicates found).
- Performed data validation and consistency checks.
- Added one new calculated column to support segmentation.
- Prepared the data for PivotTable analysis and Power BI.

  ## 📈 Key Insights
The analysis revealed several critical patterns:
- **Overall Churn Rate**: 26.54% (1,869 customers churned out of 7,043).
- **Contract Type** is the strongest churn predictor:
  - Month-to-month contracts had a very high churn rate (42.7%).
  - Two-year contracts had the lowest churn rate (only 2.8%).
- **Tenure** plays a major role:
  - New customers (low tenure) churn at a significantly higher rate.
  - Loyal customers (high tenure) are much more stable.
- **Internet Service**: Fiber optic users experienced higher churn compared to DSL users.
- **Payment Method**: Customers using **Electronic check** had the highest churn rate, while automatic payments (Bank transfer & Credit card) showed better retention.
- **Monthly Charges**: Higher monthly charges are positively correlated with churn.
- Other factors like gender, patner, dependants and senior citizen status showed relatively lower impact compared to contract and tenure.

## 💡 Business Recommendations
Based on the insights, here are actionable recommendations for the telecom company:
1. **Prioritize Contract Conversion**  
   Aggressively promote one-year and two-year contracts with discounts or added benefits to reduce month-to-month customers.

2. **Targeted Retention for New Customers**  
   Implement onboarding programs, welcome offers, and regular check-ins during the first 6–12 months to lower early churn.

3. **Review Fiber Optic Service**  
   Investigate service quality, pricing, and customer complaints related to Fiber optic to improve satisfaction.

4. **Promote Automatic Payment Methods**  
   Offer incentives (e.g., discounts or bill credits) for customers to switch from Electronic check to automatic payments.

5. **Pricing & Bundling Strategy**  
   Review pricing tiers and create attractive bundles to reduce perceived high monthly charges.

6. **Loyalty Programs**  
   Introduce loyalty rewards and retention campaigns focused on high-tenure customers to maintain their satisfaction.

   **Positive Findings:**
- The company has been **successful in upselling additional services**. A good number of customers subscribed to **Streaming TV** and **Streaming Movies**, indicating effective bundling and cross-selling strategies.
- The company performed well in providing **Tech Support**. Customers who subscribed to Tech Support showed relatively better retention, suggesting that the technical assistance offered is meeting customer needs.
- Customers with **Online Security**, **Online Backup**, and **Device Protection** reflect the company’s success in offering valuable service packages.
